Project Proposal
September 15, 2024 Marks: 15%

The project proposal outlines our research on ML-Enhanced Data Loss Prevention, including the problem statement, objectives, methodology, and expected outcomes. This milestone establishes the foundation for our research and sets the direction for subsequent stages of development.

Key deliverables include:

  • Problem statement and justification
  • Research objectives and questions
  • Literature review summary
  • Proposed methodology and technical approach
  • Project timeline and resource allocation
Completed
Progress Presentation 1
November 10, 2024 Marks: 20%

The first progress presentation focuses on our initial implementation of the data classification and risk scoring modules. It showcases our progress in developing the core components of the ML-Enhanced Data Loss Prevention system.

Key achievements include:

  • Implementation of the sensitive data classification using BERTopic and DeBERTa models
  • Development of the homomorphic encryption module for secure logging
  • Initial design of the user risk scoring framework
  • Preliminary testing results and performance metrics
Completed
Progress Presentation 2
January 25, 2025 Marks: 20%

The second progress presentation focuses on our implementation of the endpoint security and steganalysis modules. It demonstrates the integration of these components with the previously developed modules.

Key achievements include:

  • Development of the webcam-based login security system
  • Implementation of phishing detection using machine learning
  • Integration of deep learning-based steganalysis for both spatial and DCT domains
  • System-wide integration and preliminary end-to-end testing
Completed
Final Assessment
March 15, 2025 Marks: 35%

The final assessment involves a comprehensive evaluation of our complete ML-Enhanced Data Loss Prevention system. It includes a detailed presentation of all system components, their integration, and performance metrics.

Key deliverables include:

  • Fully functional DLP system with all integrated components
  • Comprehensive documentation of the system architecture and implementation
  • Detailed evaluation results and performance analysis
  • Discussion of limitations and potential future enhancements
  • Final research paper documenting the entire project
Upcoming
Viva Examination
April 5, 2025 Marks: 10%

The viva examination is an oral defense of our research project. It involves a detailed discussion with the examination panel about the theoretical foundations, methodological approach, implementation details, and research findings.

Key aspects include:

  • In-depth understanding of the research domain and related literature
  • Technical proficiency in ML, DLP, and cybersecurity principles
  • Ability to explain design decisions and implementation details
  • Critical analysis of results and awareness of limitations
  • Discussion of potential future research directions
